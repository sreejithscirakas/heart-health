Understanding Heart Disease

Key Risk Factors

  • High Blood Pressure: Strains your heart and arteries.
  • High Cholesterol: Leads to plaque buildup in arteries.
  • Smoking: Damages the lining of your arteries.
  • Diabetes: Increases the risk of heart disease.
  • Obesity: Puts extra strain on your heart.
  • Physical Inactivity: Weakens the heart muscle.
  • Unhealthy Diet: Leads to obesity and high cholesterol.

What is Heart Disease?

Heart disease refers to various conditions that affect the heart's structure and function. The most common type is coronary artery disease, which can lead to heart attacks.

Reduce Your Risk

Understanding these risk factors can help you make lifestyle changes to reduce your risk.

Diagnostic Services

  • Electrocardiograms (EKG/ECG): Measures the electrical activity of your heart to detect irregularities.
  • Echocardiograms: Uses ultrasound to create images of the heart, helping diagnose heart valve issues and heart muscle function.
  • Stress Testing: Assesses how your heart performs under physical stress.
  • Holter Monitoring: Records continuous heart activity over 24-48 hours to detect arrhythmias.
  • Cardiac Catheterization: Involves inserting a catheter into a blood vessel to diagnose and treat heart conditions.

Interventional Cardiology

Our interventional cardiology team specializes in minimally invasive procedures to treat heart disease.

  • Angioplasty and Stenting: Opens blocked arteries and inserts stents to keep them open.
  • Atherectomy: Removes plaque from the arteries.
  • Cardiac Ablation: Treats arrhythmias by destroying abnormal heart tissue.
  • Pacemaker and ICD Implantation: Devices that help manage heart rhythms.

Structural Heart Treatments

We offer advanced treatments for structural heart issues, using cutting-edge technology:

  • Transcatheter Aortic Valve Implantation (TAVI): A minimally invasive procedure to replace a diseased aortic valve.
  • Mitral Clip Repairs: A catheter-based technique to repair the mitral valve without open-heart surgery.
  • Left Atrial Appendage Closure (LAAC): Reduces stroke risk in patients with atrial fibrillation.
  • Septal Defect Closures: Minimally invasive techniques to repair holes in the heart.

Surgical Services

Our cardiovascular surgeons are experts in performing a wide range of heart surgeries:

  • Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG): Restores blood flow to the heart by bypassing blocked arteries.
  • Heart Valve Repair and Replacement: Surgical procedures to repair or replace damaged heart valves.
  • Aneurysm Repair: Surgical intervention to correct an enlarged artery.
  • Heart Transplantation: Replaces a diseased heart with a healthy donor heart in severe cases of heart failure.

Heart Failure Management

We provide comprehensive care for patients with heart failure, including:

  • Medication Management: Optimizing medications to improve heart function.
  • Device Therapy: Use of devices like pacemakers and defibrillators.
  • Lifestyle Counseling: Guidance on diet, exercise, and lifestyle  changes.
  • Advanced Therapies: Options like ventricular assist devices (VADs) and heart transplantation.

Preventive Cardiology

Preventing heart disease is a crucial part of our mission:

  • Risk Assessment: Identifying individual risk factors and creating personalized prevention plans.
  • Lifestyle Modification Programs: Support for quitting smoking, weight management, and stress reduction.
  • Nutritional Counseling: Tailored advice to help you eat a heart-healthy diet.
  • Exercise Programs: Customized plans to increase physical activity safely and effectively.

Rehabilitation  Services

Our cardiac rehabilitation program helps patients recover and thrive after a heart event or procedure:

  • Supervised Exercise Training: Safe, structured exercise to improve heart health.
  • Education and Counseling: Information on heart-healthy living and emotional support.
  • Nutritional Guidance: Support for making dietary changes to improve health outcomes.

Culturally
Tailored Services

We understand that addressing the heart health needs of our Māori and Pacific Islander communities requires culturally appropriate care and community involvement. Our services are designed to respect and incorporate Māori and Pacific Islander values, traditions, and health practices.

Whānau-Centered Care: We provide whānau-centered care that involves family members in the treatment process, ensuring support and understanding from loved ones.

Māori and Pacific Islander Health Navigators: Our Māori and Pacific Islander health navigators are available to help guide you through the healthcare system, offering personalized support and ensuring culturally sensitive care.

Community Engagement: We actively engage with Māori and Pacific Islander communities to promote heart health through educational workshops, community events, and collaborations with local iwi, hapū, and islander groups.

Tailored Prevention Programs: Our prevention programs are tailored to address the specific risk factors prevalent in Māori and Pacific Islander communities, emphasizing traditional diets, physical activities, and holistic wellness practices.

Dr. Rajesh (Raj) Nair is a leading interventional and structural cardiologist with expertise in minimally invasive heart procedures like TAVI, MitraClip, and complex congenital heart interventions.
